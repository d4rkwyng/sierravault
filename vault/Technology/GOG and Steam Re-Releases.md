# GOG and Steam Re-Releases

<small style="color: gray">Last updated: July 13, 2026</small>

## Overview

The legal commercial path to playing Sierra adventure games on modern systems runs primarily through two digital storefronts: **GOG.com** (DRM-free) and **Steam**. GOG carries the majority of the Sierra adventure catalogue with pre-packaged DOSBox or ScummVM configurations; Steam carries a smaller subset plus most post-2014 re-releases.[^ref-1][^ref-2]

This page is a snapshot of legal availability as of **May 2026**. The picture changes as Activision Blizzard (now Microsoft Gaming, since the October 2023 close) periodically adjusts back-catalogue licensing — see [[Corporate Lineage|Corporate Lineage]] for the IP-rights context.

**Verification protocol:** Never trust a GOG link in a citation without first checking the title is actually available — Brave/Google search `site:gog.com "Game Title"` is the recommended verification step.[^ref-3]

## GOG.com Sierra catalogue

GOG carries roughly **40 distinct Sierra-published titles** as of May 2026, mostly bundled into thematic collections. The packages typically ship with DOSBox preconfigured for AGI titles, ScummVM for SCI-engine titles, and native Windows binaries for SCI32 and later. Each title has been patched for compatibility with modern Windows 10/11, macOS, and Linux through GOG's compatibility-shim layer.[^ref-4]

### What's NOT on GOG

- **Most acquired-studio non-flagship titles** — Many smaller Sierra-published games (Stunt Flyer, A.G.E., Bargon Attack, etc.) are not available from any official storefront.
- **Most Hoyle entries** — The franchise's licensing situation makes the back-catalogue scarce digitally.
- **Most 3D Ultra entries** — The Lionel TrainTown and Radio Control Racers titles are particularly hard to find.
- **Coktel pre-Gobliiins titles** — 20,000 Leagues, Asterix, Geisha, Cougar Force, etc.
- **Some Phoenix/AGDI fan remakes** — Available via the developers' own sites, not GOG.

For these titles, the legal preservation path runs through [[ScummVM]] + original game data (if the user owns physical copies) or, for genuinely abandoned titles, archive.org / MyAbandonware preservation copies.[^ref-5]

## Steam Sierra catalogue

Steam's Sierra coverage overlaps GOG's but is smaller. Steam carries:

### Adventure flagship series on Steam

- **King's Quest (2015)** — The Odd Gentlemen episodic remake; the only KQ entry on Steam.[^ref-6]
- **Leisure Suit Larry: Reloaded** (Replay Games HD remake of LSL1)
- **Leisure Suit Larry: Wet Dreams Don't Dry / Wet Dreams Dry Twice** (Assemble Entertainment, post-Sierra)
- **Gabriel Knight: Sins of the Fathers 20th Anniversary Edition** (Pinkerton Road remake)
- **Phantasmagoria 1 and 2** (re-release)
- **Quest for Infamy** (spiritual successor, post-Sierra)
- **Hero-U: Rogue to Redemption** (Coles)
- **Gobliiins 4, Gobliiins 5, Gobliins 6** (Pierre Gilhodes independent continuations)
- **Mage's Initiation: Reign of the Elements** (spiritual successor, Phoenix Online)
- **Homeworld Remastered Collection** and **Homeworld 3** (Gearbox)
- **Pharaoh: A New Era** (Triskell/Dotemu 2023 remake)

### Other Sierra-adjacent Steam titles

- Impressions city-builders: Caesar III, Pharaoh + Cleopatra, Zeus + Poseidon, Emperor
- Half-Life and expansion packs (Valve owns and re-releases these)
- Various Activision-revived Sierra-label digital titles (King's Quest 2015 chapters)

### Notable Steam-exclusive vs. GOG

Most modern alumni-spiritual-successor releases (Hero-U, SpaceVenture, the Pierre Gilhodes Gobliiins continuations, Pharaoh: A New Era) appear on Steam first or simultaneously with GOG. Replay Games' Leisure Suit Larry re-releases are typically Steam-and-GOG dual-released.

## Modern remakes and re-releases by license category

### Official licensed re-releases (post-1999)

- **[[1993 - Gabriel Knight - Sins of the Fathers|Gabriel Knight: Sins of the Fathers — 20th Anniversary Edition]]** (Pinkerton Road, 2014) — Officially licensed remake by original designer.[^ref-7]
- **[[2015 - King's Quest|King's Quest (2015)]]** (The Odd Gentlemen, Activision/Sierra Entertainment) — Episodic franchise reboot.[^ref-8]
- **[[2013 - Leisure Suit Larry - Reloaded|Leisure Suit Larry: Reloaded]]** (Replay Games, 2013) — Crowdfunded HD remake of LSL1 with Al Lowe's involvement.[^ref-9]
- **[[2023 - Pharaoh - A New Era|Pharaoh: A New Era]]** (Triskell Interactive / Dotemu, 2023) — Licensed remake of Pharaoh (1999).[^ref-10]

### Fan / spiritual-successor releases on commercial storefronts

Some fan-remake projects of Sierra titles are available commercially:

- **[[2008 - Quest for Glory II VGA Remake|Quest for Glory II VGA]]** (AGDI) — Free via [agdinteractive.com](https://www.agdinteractive.com), not on storefronts.
- **AGDI's King's Quest 1, 2, 3 remakes** — Free via AGDI, not on storefronts.

Fan-game policy: AGDI, Infamous Adventures, and similar fan-developer studios have always released their Sierra remakes free of charge. Commercial fan-derived titles (Quest for Infamy, Mage's Initiation, etc.) are original IP that occupies the same design space, not Sierra IP.

## Preservation gaps and recommended actions

For Sierra titles NOT available on GOG or Steam:

1. **Verify legal status** — Some are "abandonware" by community convention but technically remain under Activision Blizzard / Microsoft IP. For research/archival purposes the Internet Archive and MyAbandonware preserve copies; this is a grey area legally and ethically.
2. **Use [[ScummVM]]** if you own original game data (CDs, floppies) — Run any AGI or SCI-engine title legally on modern hardware.
3. **Watch for Activision/Microsoft licensing announcements** — Periodically the catalogue expands. The 2023 Pharaoh remake licensing suggests Microsoft is open to back-catalogue deals.
4. **Wishlist GOG dreamlist** — The [GOG dreamlist](https://www.gog.com/dreamlist) lets users vote for titles to receive licensed re-releases. Several Sierra entries have active dreamlist campaigns (Crazy Nick's Software Picks series, several Coktel titles, Manhunter, the missing Hoyle volumes).[^ref-11]

## Citation protocol for vault pages

When adding a GOG or Steam URL to a vault page's References section, follow these rules:

1. **Verify the URL resolves** by visiting it in a browser (Brave Search `site:gog.com "Game Title"` is the fastest way to find canonical URL).[^ref-3]
2. **Use the canonical short URL** — `gog.com/en/game/<slug>` for English; `gog.com/game/<slug>` is the slug-only form.
3. **Include in `## Downloads` section** under a `**Purchase / Digital Stores**` heading, not in the main References list (unless the GOG listing is the source for a factual claim, in which case both).
4. **Update on commercial-availability changes** — A title removed from GOG should be moved to the preservation section.
